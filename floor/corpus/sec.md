# SEC cybersecurity-disclosure corpus

The SEC's cybersecurity incident disclosure rule: Item 1.05 of Form 8-K and Item
106 of Regulation S-K (17 CFR 229.106), adopted in the final rule "Cybersecurity
Risk Management, Strategy, Governance, and Incident Disclosure" (Release Nos.
33-11216; 34-97989; effective September 5, 2023). United States federal
regulations are public-domain works of the federal government. Text reproduced
verbatim from the Code of Federal Regulations / the Form 8-K instructions on the
SEC's site and the Federal Register, normalized only by removing em/en dashes per
this repository's hygiene rule (none were present in the reproduced text).

<!-- chunk: SEC-Form8K-Item1.05(a) | Form 8-K Item 1.05(a) | Material cybersecurity incidents disclosure -->
(a) If the registrant experiences a cybersecurity incident that is determined by
the registrant to be material, describe the material aspects of the nature, scope,
and timing of the incident, and the material impact or reasonably likely material
impact on the registrant, including its financial condition and results of
operations.

<!-- chunk: SEC-Form8K-Item1.05(b) | Form 8-K Item 1.05(b) | Information not determined or unavailable at the time of filing -->
(b) To the extent that the information called for in paragraph (a) of this item is
not determined or is unavailable at the time of the required filing, the registrant
shall include a statement to this effect in the filing and then must file an
amendment to its Form 8-K filing under this Item 1.05 containing such information
within four business days after the registrant, without unreasonable delay,
determines such information or within four business days after such information
becomes available.

<!-- chunk: SEC-Form8K-Item1.05-Instruction1 | Form 8-K Item 1.05 Instruction 1 | Timing of the filing -->
Instruction 1 to Item 1.05. A registrant shall file a report under this Item 1.05
within four business days after the registrant determines that it has experienced a
material cybersecurity incident. The registrant shall provide the information
required by this Item 1.05 in accordance with Rule 12b-20, which requires the
addition of such further material information, if any, as may be necessary to make
the required statements, in light of the circumstances under which they are made,
not misleading.

<!-- chunk: SEC-Form8K-Item1.05-Instruction2 | Form 8-K Item 1.05 Instruction 2 | National security or public safety delay -->
Instruction 2 to Item 1.05. Notwithstanding General Instruction B.1. to Form 8-K,
if the United States Attorney General determines that disclosure required by
paragraph (a) of this Item 1.05 poses a substantial risk to national security or
public safety, and notifies the Commission of such determination in writing, the
registrant may delay providing the disclosure required by this Item 1.05 for a time
period specified by the Attorney General, up to 30 days following the date when the
disclosure required by this Item 1.05 was otherwise required to be provided.

<!-- chunk: SEC-17CFR229.106(a) | 17 CFR 229.106(a) (Regulation S-K Item 106) | Definition of a cybersecurity incident -->
(a) Definitions. For purposes of this section: Cybersecurity incident means an
unauthorized occurrence, or a series of related unauthorized occurrences, on or
conducted through a registrant's information systems that jeopardizes the
confidentiality, integrity, or availability of a registrant's information systems
or any information residing therein.

<!-- chunk: SEC-CF-CDI-104B.01 | Division of Corporation Finance C&DI Question 104B.01 | When the four-business-day clock starts -->
Summary (not verbatim): in its Compliance and Disclosure Interpretations on Form
8-K Item 1.05, the SEC's Division of Corporation Finance explains that the
four-business-day filing deadline runs from the registrant's determination that a
cybersecurity incident is MATERIAL, not from the date the incident is discovered.
A registrant must make that materiality determination without unreasonable delay
after discovery. The guidance is interpretive: it confirms the trigger is the
materiality determination, which is why the Deadline Room anchors the SEC clock at
the materiality-determination instant rather than at incident occurrence. The exact
question-and-answer text is published on the SEC's Corporation Finance C&DI page;
this chunk is labelled a summary because that precise wording is not reproduced
verbatim here.
