# NYDFS Part 500 breach-notification corpus

New York State Department of Financial Services Cybersecurity Requirements for
Financial Services Companies, 23 NYCRR Part 500, as amended (the Second Amendment
took effect November 1, 2023). Section 500.17 is the notice-to-the-superintendent
duty the Deadline Room grounds the NYDFS filing against. New York State regulations
are public legal material. Text reproduced verbatim from the official 23 NYCRR Part
500 as published by NYDFS, normalized only by removing em/en dashes per this
repository's hygiene rule (none were present in the reproduced text).

<!-- chunk: NYDFS-23NYCRR500.17(a) | 23 NYCRR 500.17(a) | Notice of a cybersecurity event to the superintendent -->
(a) Notice of a cybersecurity event. (1) Each covered entity shall notify the
superintendent electronically in the form set forth on the department's website as
promptly as possible but in no event later than 72 hours after determining that a
cybersecurity event has occurred at the covered entity, its affiliates, or a third
party service provider that meets any of the following criteria:

(i) the cybersecurity event impacts the covered entity and notice of it is required
to be provided to any government body, self-regulatory agency, or any other
supervisory body;

(ii) the cybersecurity event has a reasonable likelihood of materially harming any
material part of the normal operation(s) of the covered entity; or

(iii) the cybersecurity event results in the deployment of ransomware within a
material part of the covered entity's information systems.

(2) Each covered entity shall promptly provide to the superintendent any
information requested regarding such cybersecurity event. The covered entity shall
have a continuing obligation to update and supplement the information provided.

<!-- chunk: NYDFS-23NYCRR500.01(d) | 23 NYCRR 500.1(d) | Definition of a cybersecurity event -->
Summary (not verbatim): 23 NYCRR 500.1 defines a "Cybersecurity Event" as any act
or attempt, successful or unsuccessful, to gain unauthorized access to, disrupt, or
misuse an information system or information stored on such information system. The
exact definitional text is in the definitions section of 23 NYCRR Part 500 as
published by NYDFS; this chunk is labelled a summary because that precise wording is
not reproduced verbatim here.
